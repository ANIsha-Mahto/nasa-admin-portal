About NASA

NASA, the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, is the United States government agency responsible for the nation's civilian space program and for aeronautics and aerospace research.

Since its establishment in 1958, NASA has led the world in space exploration, scientific discovery, and technological innovation. From the Apollo moon landings to the Mars rovers and the International Space Station, NASA continues to push the boundaries of human knowledge and capability.

Astronomy Picture of the Day

Andromeda Galaxy with Satellite Galaxies

The Andromeda Galaxy, also known as M31, is the closest large spiral galaxy to our Milky Way. This stunning image shows Andromeda along with two of its satellite galaxies, M32 and M110. Andromeda is on a collision course with our galaxy, expected to merge in about 4.5 billion years.
